Spot the soft ask

Below are three soft asks overheard in real calls. Tap each one to see what makes it soft — then pick one to rewrite as a strong ask anchored to the pro's stated outcome.

Soft Ask #1

"I mean, we could bump it up to like $250 or something and just see what happens?"

Context: The pro said she wants to stay fully booked through the fall.

Tap to see what makes it soft

What makes it soft

"Like $250 or something" hedges the number — it sounds like the AE is guessing. "Just see what happens" removes any rationale entirely. The question mark turns a recommendation into a request for approval.

Missing: a confident number, a rationale tied to her goal, and the AE owning the recommendation instead of asking the pro to confirm it.

Soft Ask #2

"Would you maybe want to try a slightly higher budget this week?"

Context: The pro said he wants 3 more bookings per month to replace income from a client he lost.

Tap to see what makes it soft

What makes it soft

"Would you maybe want to" turns a recommendation into a question the pro has to answer. "Slightly higher" isn't a number. "This week" frames it as temporary rather than a real plan to replace the income he lost.

Missing: a specific number, anchor to replacing the lost income, and the AE stating the recommendation instead of asking permission.

Soft Ask #3

"I don't know, maybe $300 could work? What do you think?"

Context: The pro said she wants to grow her business enough to hire a second employee within 6 months.

Tap to see what makes it soft

What makes it soft

"I don't know" signals the AE hasn't decided either. "Maybe $300 could work" hedges the number and frames it as a possibility rather than a recommendation. "What do you think?" hands the decision back to the pro entirely — the AE has abdicated the role of advisor.

Missing: all three — outcome anchor, confident number without hedging, and AE ownership of the recommendation.
