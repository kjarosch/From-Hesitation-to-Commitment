What Success Looks Like for Your AEs

This program was built from what you and your peers told us — through Q1 calibration data and conversations with Sales leadership — about where calls actually fall apart. The gaps weren't product knowledge. They were application: AEs knowing the product but struggling to use it in the moment when a pro pushes back, rushes to pricing, or gives a soft yes that goes dark. These are the six behaviors we're building toward.

1
Ask layered discovery questions
Surface two things at once — the present situation and its implication, the structure and the capacity, the pain and its cost — instead of running a checklist.
2
Recommend the right package using the pro's own words
Package recommendations that are anchored in what the pro actually said — not features, not gut feel — so the fit is self-evident to the pro.
3
Talk about lead cost and ROI as ranges, not exact prices
Use Mega Dashboard market averages to frame cost in context of the pro's revenue goals — without quoting a price that becomes the thing they push back on.
4
Spot when a "yes" isn't really a yes
Recognize callbacks, vague timing, and polite acceptance as commitment gaps — not closes. This was the highest-impact gap surfaced in Q1 calibration: deals going dark after what felt like a yes.
5
Stay in discovery when a pro wants to skip to pricing
Hold diagnostic discipline under pressure — not stalling, but making sure a recommendation is built on something real before the pro gets a number.
6
Close with a specific next step
Convert hesitation with a clarifying move and a concrete commitment — not an open-ended callback that has no follow-through path.

Your Role as a Manager

Sales Manager Discussion Guide
The guide is built to make coaching this work concrete — not just "reinforce the content" but a specific path for every scenario your AEs will run into.
  • Simulation scorecards: Every AE sends you their Marcus capstone results via Slack. The guide gives you structured 1:1 prompts tied directly to their score and typed responses — so the conversation starts with what they actually wrote, not a generic debrief.
  • Course-by-course coaching prompts: Questions to check application, not just completion. "Walk me through how you'd explain weekly budget to a pro who's hesitating" — not "did you finish the course?"
  • Pass/fail conversation starters: Different outcomes call for different coaching. The guide covers 3/3, 2/3, 1/3, and Did Not Complete so you know how to celebrate strong attempts and coach struggle without it feeling punitive.
